About Babcock Ranch

Babcock Ranch, adjacent to the 73,000 acre Babcock Ranch Preserve, is a master-planned community under development on the Lee and Charlotte County line in Southwest Florida. The planned community was approved as part of a public-private partnership with the State of Florida and local governments. With 18,000 acres of land, Babcock Ranch will grow to accommodate 19,000 homes and 50,000 residents when completed.

The community is considered to be the first fully solar powered city in the nation. A large solar farm several miles north of the town and a network of rooftop solar panels on commercial buildings are intended to send more renewable power into the Florida electrical grid than the city consumes.

The center of town, also known as Founders Square, has been configured to host festivals and events, and features places to eat, bicycle and watersports rentals, and a superb view of Lake Babcock. Bands frequently play at Founders Square, and weekend nights are filled with a variety of food trucks frequented by local residents and nearby communities.

Local businesses in Founders Square include the Lee Health Healthy Life Center, The Lakehouse Kitchen & Bar Restaurant, Slater's Goods & Provisions, The Hatchery (a coworking and office space located above Slater's), and Bloom Academy pre-kindergarten school.

Schools at Babcock Ranch include Babcock Neighborhood School offering a "greenSTEAM" education that integrates Science, Technology, Engineering, the Arts and Mathematics for grades K-10. Opening for the 2022-2023 school year, Babcock High School will sit just south of Babcock Neighborhood School. At this state-of-the-art, project-based high school, Babcock Ranch students will continue their STEAM-focused education. Located at Founders Square, Bloom Academy is a pre-kindergarten school.
